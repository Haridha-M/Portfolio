Project 1

Opinion mining , also known as sentiment analysis, provides a quick and comprehensive method for monitoring public sentiments. It considers words, retweets, hashtags, and emoticons to analyze opinions. Tourism is a popular topic on social media, and this framework can predict users' intention to visit a place. It utilizes untrained rules, random variables, and an evidence set to create a Bayesian Network. The trained model can then evaluate new Tweets, and it can be continuously retrained to improve its robustness.

Project 2

Short texts, such as tweets and headlines, have gained popularity on the internet. While topic modeling techniques like LDA are successful with long documents, they struggle with short texts due to limited word co-occurrence information. To address this, we propose a Targeted Analysis Model that uses Reinforcement Learning to extract relevant documents in a specific domain and perform topic generation. The model achieves a 25.7% improvement in the F1 score using Twitter data. We design a reward function to prevent false propagation from non-targeted documents and update the policy to obtain more relevant documents.
